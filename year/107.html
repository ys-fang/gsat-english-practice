As technology advances and industries evolve, professionals must continuously update their skills and knowledge to remain relevant in their careers. This shift has led to the emergence of various educational approaches designed to support adult learners.

The benefits of lifelong learning extend beyond career advancement. Studies show that continuous learning helps maintain cognitive function as we age and provides personal satisfaction. It also enables individuals to adapt to societal changes and contribute more effectively to their communities. In an era of rapid technological change, the ability to learn continuously has become as important as any specific skill or knowledge.

第39至42題

Plastic pollution has become one of the most visible and persistent environmental problems of our time. Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste enter our oceans, threatening marine life and ecosystems. The problem is particularly acute because most plastics are not biodegradable and can persist in the environment for hundreds of years.

The sources of plastic pollution are diverse and widespread. Single-use items like bags, bottles, straws, and food packaging constitute a significant portion of plastic waste. However, microplastics—tiny plastic particles less than 5 millimeters in diameter—have emerged as an equally concerning issue. These particles come from the breakdown of larger plastic items, synthetic clothing fibers, and cosmetic products.

The impact of plastic pollution on marine ecosystems is devastating. Sea creatures often mistake plastic debris for food, leading to malnutrition, internal injuries, and death. Microplastics have been found in fish, shellfish, and even table salt, raising concerns about potential health effects on humans. Additionally, plastic waste disrupts marine habitats and can transport invasive species across ocean basins.

Addressing plastic pollution requires a multi-faceted approach involving governments, businesses, and individuals. Some countries have banned single-use plastics or implemented plastic bag taxes. Companies are developing biodegradable alternatives and improving recycling programs. Individuals can contribute by reducing their plastic consumption, properly disposing of plastic waste, and supporting sustainable products. However, solving this global problem will require unprecedented cooperation and innovation across all sectors of society.

第43至46題

The gig economy, characterized by short-term contracts and freelance work rather than permanent jobs, has experienced tremendous growth in recent years. Enabled by digital platforms and mobile apps, this economic model connects service providers with customers for various tasks ranging from food delivery and ride-sharing to professional consulting and creative services.

For workers, the gig economy offers unprecedented flexibility and autonomy. Individuals can choose when, where, and how much they work, allowing them to balance multiple commitments or pursue other interests. This flexibility is particularly attractive to students, parents with young children, and those seeking supplemental income. Additionally, the gig economy provides opportunities for people to monetize their skills and assets, whether it's driving their car, renting out their home, or offering specialized expertise.

However, gig work also presents significant challenges and uncertainties. Unlike traditional employees, gig workers typically do not receive benefits such as health insurance, paid leave, or retirement contributions. Their income can be unpredictable, varying based on demand, competition, and platform policies. Furthermore, gig workers often bear additional costs such as vehicle maintenance, insurance, and equipment that would typically be covered by employers.

The rise of the gig economy has sparked debates about worker rights and social protection systems. Policymakers are grappling with questions about how to classify gig workers and ensure they have access to adequate benefits and protections. Some jurisdictions have implemented new regulations requiring platforms to provide certain benefits to their workers, while others are exploring portable benefit systems that would follow workers across different gigs and platforms. As the gig economy continues to evolve, finding the right balance between flexibility and security remains a key challenge.
