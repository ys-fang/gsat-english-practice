第35-38題為題組

The concept of "digital detox" has gained significant attention in recent years as people become increasingly aware of their dependence on digital devices. A digital detox refers to a period when an individual refrains from using electronic devices such as smartphones, computers, and social media platforms to reduce stress and focus on real-life interactions.

Research shows that excessive screen time can lead to various health issues, including eye strain, sleep disturbances, and increased anxiety. Many people report feeling overwhelmed by the constant stream of notifications, emails, and social media updates. The fear of missing out (FOMO) has become a common phenomenon, causing individuals to check their devices compulsively throughout the day.

Digital detox programs range from short weekend breaks to extended retreats lasting several weeks. Participants typically engage in outdoor activities, meditation, reading, or face-to-face conversations. Many report improved sleep quality, better concentration, and stronger personal relationships after completing a digital detox program.

However, critics argue that complete disconnection from digital technology is unrealistic in today's interconnected world. They suggest that rather than avoiding technology altogether, people should learn to use it more mindfully and establish healthy boundaries around their digital consumption.

第39-42題為題組

Microplastics, tiny plastic particles smaller than 5 millimeters, have become a global environmental concern. These particles originate from various sources, including the breakdown of larger plastic items, synthetic clothing fibers, and microbeads in personal care products. Scientists have discovered microplastics in oceans, rivers, soil, air, and even in the food we eat and water we drink.

The widespread presence of microplastics poses potential risks to both environmental and human health. Marine animals often mistake these particles for food, leading to malnutrition and digestive problems. When humans consume contaminated seafood or water, microplastics can accumulate in our bodies. Although the long-term health effects are still being studied, researchers are concerned about potential impacts on the immune system and cellular function.

Addressing the microplastics problem requires a multi-faceted approach. Governments are implementing policies to reduce single-use plastics and promote recycling programs. Industries are developing biodegradable alternatives and improving waste management systems. Individuals can contribute by choosing products with minimal plastic packaging, properly disposing of plastic waste, and supporting businesses that prioritize environmental sustainability.

Recent technological innovations offer hope for solutions. Scientists are developing new materials that can capture microplastics from water systems, and some researchers are exploring the use of bacteria that can break down plastic materials. However, experts emphasize that prevention is still the most effective strategy for addressing this global challenge.

第43-46題為題組

The sharing economy has revolutionized the way people access goods and services. This economic model, facilitated by digital platforms, allows individuals to share assets, skills, or services with others for monetary compensation. Popular examples include ride-sharing services like Uber, accommodation platforms like Airbnb, and freelance marketplaces such as Upwork.

The growth of the sharing economy has been driven by several factors. Technological advancements, particularly smartphone apps and digital payment systems, have made it easier for people to connect and conduct transactions. Economic pressures, including rising living costs and stagnant wages, have motivated people to seek additional income sources. Additionally, changing consumer preferences favor access over ownership, especially among younger generations who value flexibility and sustainability.

While the sharing economy offers numerous benefits, it also presents challenges. Traditional businesses face increased competition from these platforms, sometimes leading to regulatory conflicts. Workers in the sharing economy often lack the employment protections and benefits enjoyed by traditional employees. Quality control and safety standards can be inconsistent across different platforms and service providers.

Looking forward, the sharing economy is likely to continue expanding into new sectors. However, its long-term success will depend on addressing current challenges, including developing appropriate regulatory frameworks, ensuring fair working conditions, and maintaining user trust through improved safety and quality measures.
